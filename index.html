Structural & Foundation Issues
Some of the biggest concerns with new home owners is the structure and foundation of the home they are purchasing. Almost every house has a foundational "settling" of some kind and most strutures have minor hairline cracks. Many of these issues are not a concern but some may indicate a more serious problem. Hiring a professional who knows the difference is vital to ensure you are moving into a safe and secure home.
Electrical Issues
Old wiring is a huge fire hazard and many homes built before the 1960's have outdated electrical systems that don't meet today's code and standards. In many cases, home buyers are lead to believe their electrical systems are fine, only to find out later that they have to knock down and open up walls in order to replace these outdated electrical systems. It's an extremely expensive project which is why your home inspector should be able to give you all the information necessary before you move forward with the purchase of a home that may have electrical issues.
Water Damage
Small water stains on the ceiling, gutters flooding outside areas near the home. The roof may be leaking and the basement may be flooding. Water is and will continue to infiltrate the home and knowing where the source is coming from is extremely important. A simple water stain in the basement could be originating from the roof and most home inspectors miss these key details. There can be no second guessing when it comes to the safety of your home. This is why it's important to hire professionals who can spot these key details.
