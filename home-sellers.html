Tips for Sellers

Almost all purchase contracts for homes sold today include a home inspection contingency clause: a provision to allow the buyers to hire a profession home inspector of their choosing to thoroughly evaluate the home for any major problems.

According to ASHI ( American Society of Home Inspectors), a basic home inspection includes an evaluation of 10 different areas of the home: structure, exterior, roofing system, plumbing system, electrical system, heating system, air conditioning system, interior, insulation and ventilation, and fireplaces.

Let Right Path Home Inspections prepare you for the road ahead. Let us give you the understanding you need so you can get the results you deserve.

“Take the Right Steps”

If you are thinking of putting your home on the market, a prelist inspection is always a good idea. It will help get your home ready to list by pointing out issues that you may not know existed and can be caught before they turn into a major issue. This type of inspection will appeal to potential buyers by giving them a sense of confidence and reassurance, knowing that a seller did their due diligence before putting a home on the market. It should be understood that many home buyers, especially first-time home buyers, can or will be skeptical before making this major investment. A prelist inspection may be the difference between your home sale falling through or selling your property at top dollar.
